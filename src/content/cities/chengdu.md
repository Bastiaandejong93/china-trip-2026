## Details
Chengdu is the capital of Sichuan Province, famous for its giant pandas, spicy cuisine, and relaxed teahouse culture. The city balances ancient traditions with modern development.

## Physical Geography
Chengdu lies in the fertile Sichuan Basin, surrounded by mountains. The climate is humid subtropical with mild winters and hot, humid summers. The region is known as the 'Land of Abundance' for its agricultural productivity.

## Political Context
As the capital of Sichuan Province, Chengdu is a major economic and cultural center in western China. It's a hub for finance, technology, and transportation, with growing international connections.

## People & Culture
Chengdu's 21 million residents are known for their relaxed, pleasure-seeking lifestyle. The city has a strong teahouse culture and is considered one of China's most livable cities with a slower pace than coastal metropolises.
