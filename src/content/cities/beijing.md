## Details
Beijing is the political and cultural center of China. From the immense Forbidden City and the winding Great Wall to the narrow hutongs, it's a city that commands respect and awe at every turn.

## Physical Geography
Beijing lies on the North China Plain, backed by mountains to the north and west. The climate is continental with hot, humid summers and cold, dry winters. Air quality varies, with pollution worse in winter due to heating systems.

## Political Context
As China's capital, Beijing is the seat of the central government and Communist Party. It's a direct-controlled municipality with provincial-level status, hosting all major political institutions and foreign embassies.

## People & Culture
Beijing's 21.5 million residents include a large migrant population from across China. The city has a distinct northern culture with a love of noodles, a direct communication style, and pride in its imperial heritage.
