## Details
Shanghai is a dazzling blend of East and West, old and new. From the historic Bund with its colonial architecture to the towering skyscrapers of Lujiazui, the city pulses with energy.

## Physical Geography
Shanghai sits on the Yangtze River Delta, a flat coastal plain with numerous waterways. The climate is humid subtropical with hot, humid summers and mild winters. The city's elevation is near sea level, making it vulnerable to typhoons and flooding.

## Political Context
Shanghai is a direct-controlled municipality, one of four in China with provincial-level status. It serves as the country's financial and commercial hub, hosting the Shanghai Stock Exchange and numerous multinational headquarters.

## People & Culture
With over 26 million residents, Shanghai is China's most populous city. It's highly cosmopolitan, attracting migrants from across China and expatriates worldwide. The city has a fast-paced, business-oriented culture.
