## Details
Dali is a historic town in Yunnan Province, home to the Bai ethnic minority. Nestled between Erhai Lake and the Cangshan Mountains, it offers stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

## Physical Geography
Dali sits at 2,000 meters elevation in Yunnan Province, between Erhai Lake and the 4,000-meter Cangshan Mountains. The climate is mild year-round with distinct wet and dry seasons, earning it the nickname 'Spring City of the South.'

## Political Context
Dali is the capital of the Dali Bai Autonomous Prefecture in Yunnan. The region has special autonomy to preserve Bai culture and language, balancing tourism development with cultural conservation.

## People & Culture
The Bai people are the dominant ethnic group, known for their distinctive architecture, tie-dye textiles, and the 'Three-Course Tea' ceremony. The old town has become a popular destination for domestic tourists and digital nomads.
