## Details
Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its quartz-sandstone pillars. The landscape, which inspired the floating mountains in Avatar, offers breathtaking views.

## Physical Geography
Zhangjiajie sits in the Wulingyuan Scenic Area of northwestern Hunan Province. The region features over 3,000 sandstone pillars, some reaching 200+ meters. The subtropical climate brings frequent mist and rain, creating ethereal landscapes.

## Political Context
Zhangjiajie is a prefecture-level city in Hunan Province. Since becoming a UNESCO site in 1992 and gaining fame from Avatar (2009), it has transformed into a major tourist destination with significant infrastructure development.

## People & Culture
The area is home to the Tujia and Miao ethnic minorities, who maintain traditional customs and architecture. Tourism has brought economic growth but also challenges in preserving local culture and environment.
